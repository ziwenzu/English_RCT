## **Food**

Czechia has some superb national dishes: my favourite is *šulánky s mákem*, a doughy, sweet dish made from potatoes sprinkled with poppy seeds, sugar and butter. Pub/restaurant [Lokál U Caipla](https://lokal-ucaipla.ambi.cz/en/) near the cathedral is the place to go fortraditional classics such as *svíčková na smetaně* – sliced beef in a creamy sauce with bread dumplings, whipped cream and cranberry sauce. Hearty plates like this go perfectly with a cold pilsner, and here there are three pouring styles to try: the *hladinka* has a thick foamy head but is mainly beer, the *šnyt* is predominantly dense foam with a little beer and the *mlíko* (milk) is almost entirely creamy foam with a tiny amount of beer. Those not into beer needn’t worry: Brno has a great coffee scene. [Spolek](https://www.spolek.net/) is a minimalist place with no piped music, and [Buchta C](https://www.facebook.com/buchtac/) is a modern espresso bar with delicious Czech-style *buchty* (sweet filled buns).

## **Inspiration**

![Villa Tugendhat.](https://i.guim.co.uk/img/media/1cdf36316c5a4106bab2baf6c07f331c350b81d0/0_0_6048_3628/master/6048.jpg?width=445&dpr=1&s=none&crop=none)[View image in fullscreen](https://www.theguardian.com/travel/2023/may/04/a-locals-guide-to-brno-czech-republic-how-do-you-like-your-pilsner-poured#img-2)

Villa Tugendhat. Photograph: CTK/Alamy

There’s lots about the city’s culture and heritage at [Muzeum Mêsta Brna](https://www.gotobrno.cz/en/place/brno-city-museum-muzeum-mesta-brna/) (Brno City Museum), Romany culture at [Muzeum Romské Kultury](https://www.gotobrno.cz/en/place/museum-of-romani-culture-muzeum-romske-kultury/) or the Moravia region in [Moravská Galerie v Brně](https://moravska-galerie.cz/). [Villa Tugendhat](https://www.tugendhat.eu/en/) is Czechia’s only monument of modern architecture and is on the Unesco world heritage list: tours need to be booked well in advance, as they sell out quickly. Brno is also a creative city, and being an actor, I have to mention our incredible theatres. The ornate pink-hued[ Husa na Provázku](https://www.provazek.cz/) (Goose on a String) puts on fantastic plays with English subtitles. [Feste](https://www.divadlofeste.cz/) is an independent theatre group that tackles political and social issues. They do perform in Czech but [Industra](https://industra.space/), the place where they usually perform, is worth checking out. It’s an industrial-looking creative space that promotes projects with positive social, cultural and economic impact – art exhibitions, plays, cinema and educational workshops.

The coolest neighbourhood is Kamenná Kolonie (stone colony), a scattering of 130 traditional houses in an old quarry by the Svratka River. In the past it was a working-class area inhabited by brickyard workers, but it became an enclave for artists and bohemians in the 1970s. [Nová Zbrojovka](https://novazbrojovka.cz/) is another interesting place: a former munitions factory is being transformed into a modern community with flats, office space and amenities.

## **Green space**

![The karst landscape north of Brno has spectacular caves.](https://i.guim.co.uk/img/media/e4be8d9b7a38d09598b7b1e32710e4fd19e17f46/0_0_4923_2953/master/4923.jpg?width=445&dpr=1&s=none&crop=none)[View image in fullscreen](https://www.theguardian.com/travel/2023/may/04/a-locals-guide-to-brno-czech-republic-how-do-you-like-your-pilsner-poured#img-3)

The karst landscape north of Brno has spectacular caves. Photograph: CCat82/Getty Images

The best way to explore Brno is by bike. The city is surrounded by nature, and there are a lot of green spaces in the centre too. The Denisovy Gardens, close to St Peter and Paul’s cathedral, is for me the most beautiful. About 20 minutes’ drive away, the lake created by Brno dam is a lovely place to spend the day reading alone or with family and friends: kids love the fully functioning steamboat. The Moravian karst landscape, north of the city and accessible by public transport, has spectacular caves: the 130-metre-deep Macocha Abyss ([booking essential](https://visit.caves.cz/cave/punkevni-jeskyne)) is one of Czechia’s natural wonders. Mountain bikers should check out the area around the small town of Jedovnice for trails that snake through the luscious Czech landscape.

## **Nightlife**

![Super Panda Circus.](https://i.guim.co.uk/img/media/055829c46e616fca14a62664fc81cea85786b037/0_224_6720_4032/master/6720.jpg?width=445&dpr=1&s=none&crop=none)[View image in fullscreen](https://www.theguardian.com/travel/2023/may/04/a-locals-guide-to-brno-czech-republic-how-do-you-like-your-pilsner-poured#img-4)

Super Panda Circus has performers telling stories. Photograph: Kevin Rushby/The Guardian

Brno is a university city, so it comes alive at night. I’ll often spend an evening on the wooden deck at Goose on a String’s lovely internal patio. My favourite spot for a beer is [Pivnice U Poutníka](https://www.facebook.com/pivniceupoutnika), a traditional beer hall with a wide selection of ales. For live music, [Alterna](https://www.alterna.cz/), [Metro](https://www.metromusic.cz/), [Sono](https://www.sonomusicclub.cz/) and [Fleda](https://www.fleda.cz/) host nights from dance and electro to international performers and local bands. And no one should leave Brno without visiting [Super Panda Circus](https://www.superpandacircus.cz/), an eccentric cocktail bar with performers telling stories and inviting guests to role play.
