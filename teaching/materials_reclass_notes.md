# Materials Reclassification Notes

This note documents how the title list in `IRB/Instrument.pdf` was reclassified to
match the current six-arm proposal standards.

## Authoritative local source used

- `IRB/Instrument.pdf`

## Reclassification summary

- `PRO`: 24 China-internal political items were rebuilt and verified.
- `ANTI`: 24 China-internal political items were rebuilt and verified.
- `APOL_CHINA`: 17 China-related but non-political items are currently in reserve.
- `NONCHINA_CONTROL`: 24 non-China neutral items are currently in the bank.

## Main reclassifications

- Several items that appeared inside the old "Pro-China" or "Control" blocks were moved into
  `APOL_CHINA` because they are clearly travel, food, family-travel, or urban-culture pieces.
- Cross-national, foreign-investor, military-rivalry, and international-relations pieces were
  removed from the political treatment banks during the domestic-only rescreen.
- Two borderline anti-treatment rows that still leaned on foreign reference points in their
  titles were replaced with cleaner domestic censorship items.
- Several control items remain only provisional because they are guide-style travel pieces rather
  than cleaner article-style prose.

## Domestic-only political rescreen

- The political-treatment banks were re-screened under a stricter China-internal rule.
- `PRO` and `ANTI` now exclude items whose main frame depends on foreign countries, bilateral
  diplomacy, international organizations, export markets, foreign investors, military rivalry,
  or explicit cross-national comparison.
- This stricter rule applies to `PRO` and `ANTI`, not to `APOL_CHINA` and not to
  `NONCHINA_CONTROL`.
- Source restrictions were also relaxed during rebuilding: the bank no longer has to stay inside
  the original four-outlet pool, as long as the outlet is reputable, English-language, stable,
  and suitable for article-style intervention materials.
- After the rescreen, both political banks meet the quantity target with linked, domestic-frame
  items only.
